Graduate Students

The SJSU Stem Cell Internships in Laboratory-based Learning (SCILL) Program seek students with the motivation, aptitude and independence to pursue a career in regenerative medicine and stem cell research.

Students can apply to SJSU SCILL Program online via "CSU Mentor" during the Spring semester preceding the academic year in which they wish to matriculate in the program.

The Admissions Committee reviews the applications and selects students for the SJSU SCILL Program. Final selection of candidates for the SJSU SCILL Program and internship placement will take place at an interview day at SJSU. The SJSU SCILL Mentorship Coordinator invites our top applicants for a written and oral interview in person.


Eligibility

Students seeking admissions into SJSU SCILL Program should be registered to MA or MS or MBT graduate studies in the Biological Sciences Department. Priority will be given to students that have completed a 4-year bachelor's degree with classes in molecular biology, cell biology, immunology, or related discipline, with a science GPA of above 3.0, and a general GRE score of 480 or above Verbal (or 100 or above for the TOEFL score), 680 or above Quantitative, and 4.5 or above for Analytical Writing.

Requirements

Applicants accepted to the SJSU SCILL Program must have:

  • Be/Been enrolled in a graduate program or starting your Masters in San Jose State University 2016.
  • Completed an upper division molecular/cell biology course with a lab.
  • A minimum G.P.A of (2.9 acceptable) 3.0 on a 4-point scale.
  • Three (3) completed recommendation forms.
  • A statement of purpose.
  • A resume.
  • Signed permission to contact references.

Preference will be given to those students having completed B.A./B.S. in Biology.

Our Team

The SJSU SCILL consortium is composed of SJSU faculty mentors from three departments in two colleges, 60 internship research mentors, four internship coordinators at internship host institutions, and an advisory committee composed of eight academic and industry leaders in their fields.

The SJSU SCILL Program guides students through a rigorous curriculum combining state-of-the-art training in stem cell Biology and laboratory techniques. This will allow students the opportunity to participate in an emerging scientific field in Regenerative Medicine and Stem Cell research.


+ Program Leadership

Program Director - Tzvia Abramson -Professor, SJSU

Dr. Abramson has ultimate responsibility for the program, providing administrative oversight including issues related to budget approval, student recruitment, staff employment, scheduling and staffing classes, laboratory safety and regulatory compliance. The director is also responsible for reviewing and implementing Advisory Committee decisions, as well as be the primary communication hub for Internship Host Institution (IHI) coordinators.

About

The San José State University Consortium for Stem Cell Internships in Laboratory-based Learning (SCILL) Program provides coursework towards a Master's degree in the Department of Biological Sciences (Master of Arts or Master of Biotechnology) at San Jose State University and a 12-month, full-time internship in stem cell research in one of four host institutions.

CIRM

Bridges CIRM 1.0 funded the SJSU SCILL Consortium in 2009 with $1.73m, was renewed with additional funding ($1.82m in 2012 and $0.6m in 2014) with the mission to generate a professional workforce that will enter stem cell research.
